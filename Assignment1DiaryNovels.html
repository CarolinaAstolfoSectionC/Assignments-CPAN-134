"A novel is anything that can fit between two covers", A Novela Luminosa Levrero

Diary Novels are usually written in the first person. They may be similar to diaries in format and have dated entries, but they may not. The diary reference might come in other shapes and forms, such as a tendency to explore the first person in a tentative way.

One of the most interesting things about contemporary diary novels is the way the reader is always made to question if what they are reading is fact or fiction. You can never be sure if the narrative is actually a journal intime or just pure make belief.

Figuring out just what self writing can look like nowadays and testing the limits between fiction and reality are what sets today's diary novels apart.
